Amp & Volume Control

Add in a transistor amp to make your badge louder and a volume control knob.

Step 1

Get yourself the following components:

Quantity Type
1 50K Potentiometer
1 8.2K Resistor
1 10nF Capacitor
1 2N3904 Transistor

Step 2

Look at the schematic.

Make it larger in a new tab

Step 3

Unplug your antenna and headphones. Pull your battery out.

Step 4

Plan your component layout, especially the potentiometer. You can totally do this if you want:

But it looks a bit like an udder hanging off your badge. Maybe instead you'd like to drill and do a panel mount:

Much cooler, and you get to use a power tool in the process.

The next thing you're going to do is to break any litle metal tabs off the front of the potentiometer. These tabs are meant for panel mounting, but in our design we don't have a place for them. This is a hack. Grab a pair of needle nose pliers and break them off. If you don't, the potentiometer will not sit flush on the PCB.

Figure out where you can put the thing. You want to leave some space for the other components. You also want to avoid contact with other components or covering up the solder pads you'll be working with.

Flip the potentiometer over and mark around the shaft. The marked circle will be where you drill.

Finally, drill the hole that the potentiometer will go through. You will need a drill bit that is a slightly larger diameter than the shaft of the potentiometer. If you find that the larger drill bit wants to walk around the board instead of drilling, start with a smaller bit then make the hole larger with the desired drill bit size.

Step 5

Get an X-acto knife and cut through the middle of the J-TR solder jumper. There's a small trace connecting the two pads. It's a bit difficult to cut and you may need to apply some pressure while cutting. Be mindful of your fingers.

Rotate the board so the J-S jumper is facing you. Before you cut the J-S jumper look at the below picture. See the red line? There is a battery power trace there. If you cut that, your badge will die and we will have to use a wire to bring it back to life. Repeat after me: "I will not cut the battery power trace." OK good. Now go cut the J-S solder jumper.

Grab a multimeter and set it for continuity (Ohms)... The little greek Omega symbol. Put a probe on each side of the J-S solder pad. The meter should read like an open connection. It should NOT read 0 Ohms. If it does, go back to cutting. Repeat for the J-TR solder pad. If you need help, ask for help. It's important to make sure the headphone jack is completely disconnected.

Step 6

Put your potentiometer through the board and fasten the front side with a washer and retaining nut.

Step 7

This is where things start to get a little dicey. You're going to need to keep track of transistor orientation and which transistor pins you're connecting to what other components.

If you place the 2N3904 transistor on the table so the flat part is facing up like in the diagram below, pin 1 is on the left side and pin 3 on the right.

NOTE: When you start soldering do not immediately clip off excess component leads. Those leads will help you connect components together. It is much easier to bend the lead to the next hole you want to connect to than to blob a solder bridge between two holes.

We are going to be connecting pin 3 of the 2N3904 to VCC. Go ahead and solder it in. Also solder in the 10nF capacitor so that it's in a hole right next to pin 1 of the 2N3904. Stretch the other end of the capacitor as close to GND as you can. It won't go all the way. We will bridge the gap later with wire. It should look something like the below picture when you are done with this step.

Solder one end of your resistor to VCC. Solder the other to the closest pin of the potentiometer. It helps to wrap the resistor lead around the potentiometer pin before you solder it.

Flip your badge over. It should look something like this.

Gently bend the excess lead from the capacitor to pin 2 of the 2N3904. You can cut off any lead that goes beyond where it meets with pin 2 of the 2N3904. Solder those two connections together and trim off any excess leads. Once that is done it should look like the below.

Step 8

Grab some wire. You are going to run a wire from pin 2 of the 2N3904 to the pin of the potentiometer where you previously soldered the resistor. Strip a bit of sheathing off one of the ends of the wire and feed it through a hole close to pin 2 of the 2N3904.

Flip the board over and solder the wire to pin 2 of the 2N3904.

Flip the board back over, strip the other end of the wire, wrap it around the potentiometer pin a bit, then solder it.

Step 9

Grab some more wire and strip an end. Feed the stripped end through a hole next to the capacitor. Make sure you feed enough through to connect with the capacitor pin. Solder the wire end to the capacitor pin. The other end of this wire is going to a pin labled GND. Trim to length, strip the other end, and solder to GND. See below pictures for where this wire connects.

Step 10

Use a small piece of wire or component lead scrap to solder the other 2 potentiometer pins together.

Step 11

Use a longer piece of wire to connect the hole labled "S" to the lowest pin of the potentiometer. See below picture for reference. In the picture, the wire is hidden because it was tucked under the potentiometer.

Step 12

Use a longer piece of wire to connect pin 1 of the 2N3904 to the solder pad between J-TR and the headphone jack. Be sure to feed enough wire through to reach pin 1 of the 2N3904 and solder them together. To solder one of the wire ends to the solder pad, first heat the pad with the soldering iron while putting a blob of solder on it. Then grab the wire with some tweezers, melt the solder blob again, and push the wire end into the molten blob of solder.

Step 13

Last one! You're almost done. Grab another long piece of wire and connect the solder pad next to the J-S solder jumper and GND.

Step 14

Flip the board over and trim off any excess leads.

Done!

Slide the knob onto your new volume control. You now have a louder badge and can adjust the volume with the knob you added.
